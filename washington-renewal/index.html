A Washington driver's license is typically valid for 8 years. The Washington DOL usually sends a renewal notice before expiration, but renewing on time is your responsibility even if no notice arrives. You can generally begin shortly before your expiration date.

Can You Renew Online?

Many Washington drivers qualify to renew online or by mail without visiting an office. You can usually renew online if:

  • Your renewal notice does not require an in-person visit
  • You are not getting a REAL ID for the first time
  • Your name and address are current
  • You meet vision and record requirements

REAL ID, Explained

A REAL ID is a federally compliant license (marked with a star) needed to board domestic flights or enter certain federal facilities. A standard license still lets you drive legally. Switching to a REAL ID for the first time requires an in-person visit with documents.

Drivers Age 70 and Older

Washington generally requires older drivers (around age 70+) to renew in person, which may include a vision check. It's a routine safety step. Bring your glasses if you wear them, and check the Washington DOL site for the exact age and requirements, as these can change.

Common Questions

How early can I renew?
Usually shortly before your expiration date; renewing early avoids a lapse.
What if my license already expired?
You can still renew, but driving on an expired license is illegal. Renew before driving again.
Do I need a REAL ID?
Only to fly domestically or enter federal buildings without a passport. Otherwise a standard license is fine.
