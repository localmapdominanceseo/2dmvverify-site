How to Replace a Lost or Stolen Michigan Driver's License in 2026

If your Michigan driver's license is lost, stolen, or damaged, you can order a duplicate from Michigan Secretary of State for $9. Many Michigan drivers can do this online. This guide covers every step.

A Michigan driver's license replacement (also called a duplicate license) gives you a new copy of your license with the same information and expiration date as your current one. The Michigan replacement fee in 2026 is $9. Note: if your license is also close to expiring, it may make more sense to renew instead of just replacing it.

How to Replace Your Michigan Driver's License: Step-by-Step

Report theft if applicable

If your Michigan license was stolen, consider filing a police report — especially if you are concerned about identity theft. A report can also help if the license is used fraudulently.

Check online replacement eligibility

Many Michigan drivers can order a duplicate license online through Michigan Secretary of State if their name and address haven't changed. Check your eligibility before visiting an office.

Verify your identity

You will need to confirm your identity — typically your license number, date of birth, and last four digits of your Social Security number. This is done securely on the official Michigan Secretary of State website only.

Pay the Michigan replacement fee

The Michigan driver's license replacement fee is $9. Pay directly on the official Michigan Secretary of State site or at a Michigan Secretary of State office. Never pay on unofficial websites.

Receive your replacement license

Your duplicate Michigan license arrives by mail, typically within 7–14 days. You may receive a temporary paper document immediately. Your replacement has the same expiration date as your original.

Frequently Asked Questions: Michigan License Replacement

How much does it cost to replace a lost driver's license in Michigan?
The Michigan driver's license replacement fee in 2026 is $9. This gives you a duplicate of your current license with the same expiration date. Verify the current fee at Michigan Secretary of State.
Can I replace my Michigan driver's license online?
Yes — many Michigan drivers can order a duplicate license online through the Michigan Secretary of State website if their name and address haven't changed. Check eligibility on the official Michigan Secretary of State site before visiting an office.
Does a replacement Michigan license change my expiration date?
No. A replacement (duplicate) license keeps your existing expiration date. If you want a new expiration date, you need to renew your license for $18.
Can I drive while waiting for my replacement Michigan license?
Many states issue a temporary paper license immediately. Check what Michigan Secretary of State provides in Michigan — the official website will confirm whether a temporary document is available.
I found my license after ordering a replacement. Is the old one still valid?
Once a duplicate is issued, the original is typically invalidated in the Michigan Secretary of State system. Use your new replacement license. If in doubt, contact Michigan Secretary of State directly.
