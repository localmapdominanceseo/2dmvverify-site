If your Michigan driver's license is lost, stolen, or damaged, you can request a duplicate (replacement) from Michigan Secretary of State. A replacement keeps your existing expiration date — it's a copy, not a renewal.

How to Replace It

Report if stolen

If your license was stolen, consider filing a police report, especially if identity theft is a concern.

Check online eligibility

Many states let you order a duplicate online if your information hasn't changed. Check the Michigan Secretary of State site.

Verify your identity

You'll confirm identifying details. No personal data is ever entered on this guide — only on the official site.

Pay the fee

The replacement fee in Michigan is typically around $9. Verify the current amount on the official site.

Receive your duplicate

Your replacement license is mailed, usually within a couple of weeks.

Common Questions

Can I drive while waiting for my replacement?
Many states issue a temporary document. Check what Michigan provides on the official site.
Does a replacement change my expiration date?
No — a duplicate keeps your current expiration. If you're close to expiring, you may want to renew instead.
Can I get a replacement online?
Often yes, if your details are unchanged. The Michigan Secretary of State site shows current options.
