A REAL ID is a federally compliant driver's license or ID card, marked with a star in the corner. It's required to board domestic flights and enter certain federal facilities. In Michigan, REAL IDs are issued by Michigan Secretary of State.

Do You Need a REAL ID?

You need a REAL ID (or a valid passport) if you want to fly within the U.S. or enter secure federal buildings. If you don't fly and always carry a passport, a standard license still works for driving.

Documents Required

Because REAL ID rules are federal, the document categories are the same nationwide, though Michigan may accept specific versions:

REAL ID document categories. Confirm accepted versions with Michigan Secretary of State.
CategoryExamples
Identity / lawful statusU.S. passport, birth certificate, permanent resident card

Important: The first time you get a REAL ID, you must apply in person — federal law requires document verification. Bring originals or certified copies.

How to Apply

Gather your documents

Assemble the identity, SSN, and two residency documents above.

Book an appointment

Use the Michigan Secretary of State site to schedule an in-person visit, where available.

Visit the office

Bring originals. Staff verify and scan your documents.

Pay and finish

Pay the applicable fee; your REAL ID is mailed to you.

Common Questions

Is a REAL ID mandatory?
No. It's only needed to fly domestically or enter federal facilities without a passport.
Can I get a REAL ID online?
The first REAL ID generally requires an in-person visit for document verification.
How much does it cost?
In Michigan, a REAL ID typically costs the same as a standard license/renewal fee (around $18). Verify with Michigan Secretary of State.
