Can You Renew Your West Virginia Driver's License Online?

Most West Virginia drivers can renew their driver's license online without visiting a West Virginia DMV office. Online renewal is available if you meet all of the following:

  • Your renewal notice does not specify an in-person visit is required
  • You are not upgrading to a REAL ID for the first time (first-time REAL ID always requires in-person)
  • Your name and address on file with West Virginia DMV are unchanged
  • You meet the state's vision and driving record requirements
When in-person is required: First-time REAL ID applicants, drivers with a name or address change, those with a suspended or restricted license, and drivers flagged by West Virginia DMV must renew in person at a West Virginia DMV office.

How to Renew Your West Virginia Driver's License: Step-by-Step

Follow these steps to renew your West Virginia driver's license in 2026:

Check your renewal eligibility

Review your West Virginia DMV renewal notice or check the West Virginia DMV website to confirm whether you can renew online, by mail, or must visit in person. Note your license expiration date — you can usually begin renewal within the final months before expiry.

Gather required documents

For standard online renewal: have your current license number and payment ready. For in-person or REAL ID renewal: gather your identity documents, Social Security proof, and two West Virginia residency documents.

Complete your renewal

Visit the official West Virginia DMV website to renew online, or schedule an in-person appointment. Never pay renewal fees on unofficial websites — only on the official West Virginia DMV site.

Pay the renewal fee

The West Virginia driver's license renewal fee is $26.50. Payment is accepted by the West Virginia DMV online system or at the office. You will receive a temporary paper document upon completion.

Receive your new license

Your new West Virginia driver's license will arrive by mail, typically within 1–3 weeks. Your new license is valid for 5 years from the date of renewal.

REAL ID in West Virginia: What You Need to Know

A REAL ID is a federally compliant driver's license marked with a gold or black star. Since the federal enforcement deadline, you need a REAL ID (or a valid U.S. passport) to board domestic flights and enter certain federal facilities. A standard West Virginia license is still valid for driving.

To get a West Virginia REAL ID for the first time, you must visit a West Virginia DMV office in person with your identity, Social Security, and residency documents. There is no additional fee — you pay the standard $26.50 renewal cost. If you already have a West Virginia REAL ID, renewing it follows the same process as a standard renewal.

Frequently Asked Questions: West Virginia Driver's License Renewal

How much does it cost to renew a driver's license in West Virginia?
The West Virginia driver's license renewal fee in 2026 is $26.50 for a standard license valid for 5 years. Verify the current fee at West Virginia DMV before paying, as fees can change.
How long is a West Virginia driver's license valid after renewal?

Can I renew my West Virginia driver's license online?
Yes — most West Virginia drivers can renew online if their information hasn't changed, they don't need a REAL ID for the first time, and they meet eligibility requirements. Check your renewal notice or the West Virginia DMV website to confirm.
What happens if my West Virginia driver's license has already expired?
You can still renew an expired West Virginia license, but you cannot legally drive until it is renewed. Some states require additional steps for licenses expired over a certain period — check with West Virginia DMV for current rules.
How do I replace a lost or stolen West Virginia driver's license?
Order a duplicate West Virginia license through the West Virginia DMV website. The replacement fee is $10. A replacement keeps your existing expiration date.
